DBSCAN is better than K-Means in this case.

One of the advantages of DBSCAN is that it can find clusters of arbitrary shapes and sizes, unlike K-Means which assumes spherical clusters. That is why K-Means clusters the 2 clusters in the upper-left corner into 2 round clusters, instead of the expected 2 clusters along diagonol lines as DBSCAN recognizes.

DBSCAN is also robust to noise and outliers since they are not assigned to any cluster.

However, DBSCAN can be sensitive to the choice of distance metric and parameters such as epsilon, aka the radius and minimum number of points required to form a cluster.
